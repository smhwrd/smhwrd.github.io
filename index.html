PROJECTS



Since 2017 I have been working as a freelance audio professional filling various roles in productions and events. I have featured some of my most recent projects below.

LOST FOR WORDS (2020)



Developed by Simon Howard

I realised you can do a lot of very creative things with sound using game engines and I starting having a lot of exciting ideas I wanted to explore. I didn't have a developer to work with at the time so I began doing things myself. After a few short experiments I decided to create a game with minimalist graphics, maximising the experience using sound. This is where Lost for Words began. A haunting maze of endless corridors, scattered with brief moments of respite. Equipped with only a radio and cryptic guidance, can you find a way out? Lost for Words is being developed with Unity and Wwise and will be released for Windows and macOS this Autumn.

THE WAY WE WAIT (2020)



Directed by JiYoon Park

Having just moved house, a young Korean woman in Scotland learns that her grandmother is passing away. The Way We Wait is an experimental documentary exploring loss and uncertainty. Many of the sound recordings made during filming were flat and uninteresting so I completely reconstructed the audio with foley and newly recorded ambiences. Layered recordings of electronics and machinery were added to the mix to give a heightened sense of anxiety and detachment.

MY OWN PERSONAL LEBANON (2020)



Directed by Theo Panagopoulos

A documentary short in which a Greek filmmaker tries to uncover his mother's secret stories about the Lebanese civil war and in the process, his own identity. I oversaw the audio production in this project from location recording right through to post-production.

ACCEPTABLE FACE (2019)



Animated by Holly Summerson

Acceptable face is an experimental animated documentary based on interviews with LGBTQ+ people discussing the pressures of being a good example in society and also taking pride in being the opposite. I recorded the foley using only items of stationery to pull out the more surreal and playful elements in the animation style and minimised any extensive processing to keep it sounding raw and honest, in-line with the topics at hand.

RECALL REFLECTION (2019)



Sound-Walk & Film Short

An interactive story about love, death, and shared memories in the Edinburgh Old Town. A sound-walk takes you on a journey with Jimmy, an Irish widower, as he remembers the life of his wife and the time they spent together in the city. At the end of the walk is a short film retracing the journey with his wife Peggy, responding to him from the other side of death. The sound-walk is fully automated by a mobile friendly web-app using Google Maps API to guide users to different landmarks and manages audio playback based on user location. The film was produced by animating point clouds of each landmark in the soundwalk using a Leica LiDAR scanner. Both are still available online, including a newly produced film in lieu of the sound-walk.

ABOUT




I began my journey with sound at the Sonic Arts Research Centre in Belfast where I was awarded a Bachelors degree in Music Technology. Here I discovered creative programming and interactive sound, and started developing real-time responsive systems for solo performance. I moved to Scotland in 2018, completing a Masters in Sound Design at the University of Edinburgh. I continue to make a racket in the city of Glasgow, working as an audio professional on various projects including short films, animations, multimedia exhibitions and live sound.
